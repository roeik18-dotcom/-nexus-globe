# Philos Research Questions

**Status:** Living document · v1.0
**Purpose:** Single source of truth for every foundational question the Philos system depends on.
**Rule:** Before adding a new concept, idea, or feature — check which open question it answers.
If it answers none, it is not yet needed in the framework.

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|

## Layer A — Ontology
*What things ARE*

| # | Question | Status | Where defined | To close |
|---|----------|--------|---------------|----------|
| A1 | What is a Force? | 🟡 | 6-force model in analyze/route.ts (emotional, physical, id, ego, social, rational). Freudian naming (ID/EGO/SUPEREGO). | Replace Freudian naming. Ground in Self-Determination Theory (Competence / Autonomy / Relatedness) + Conservation of Resources (Hobfoll). Define each force with: origin, observable expression, resolution condition. |
| A2 | What is a Value? | 🟡 | 5 values listed: Truth, Justice, Protection, Responsibility, Dignity. Source unknown. | Explain why exactly these 5. Are they exhaustive? Are they universal across cultures? What framework generates them? Candidates: Haidt's Moral Foundations, Schwartz's Basic Human Values (10 domains), Universal Declaration of Human Rights. |
| A3 | What is a Need? | 🔴 | Not formally defined. "Need" appears in product language but has no ontology entry. | Distinguish Need from Force and from Value. SDT defines 3 basic needs (Competence, Autonomy, Relatedness). Maslow defines 5 levels. Philos must pick a framework or explain the departure. |
| A4 | What is Orientation? | 🟡 | Orientation Score (0–100) exists. Components: balanceGain, energyRecovery, collectiveDistribution, leakageRelief. Band labels: Collapse / First Stabilization / Active Recovery / Strong Orientation. | Theoretical definition of Orientation as a concept (not just a score). What does it mean for a person to be "oriented"? What is the opposite? How does this relate to Viktor Frankl's concept of meaning, SDT's autonomy, or Antonovsky's sense of coherence? |
| A5 | What is a Conflict? | 🟡 | Conflict types: blocked_feeling, analysis_paralysis, depletion, image_gap, expectation_gap, unmet_craving, regression. Generated by deriveConflict(force, direction). | The current types are clinically-framed (analysis_paralysis, blocked_feeling). Needs replacement with development-framed types consistent with the Philos Shift. What is a conflict in a growth-oriented system vs. a deficit-oriented system? |
| A6 | What is a Resource? | 🟡 | Partially defined as dimensionCapacity (how much help could theoretically arrive). Also: Physical / Emotional / Rational dimensions as resources (Layer 1). | Formal definition: is a resource only external support, or does it include internal capacity? Conservation of Resources theory (Hobfoll) defines 4 categories: objects, conditions, personal characteristics, energies. Does Philos use all 4? |
| A7 | What is a Constraint? | 🔴 | Mentioned in the Orientation Engine (§6: eligibility, coverage, non-depletion, consent) as feasibility constraints on plans. Not defined as a standalone ontology concept. | Define Constraint as a first-class concept: what types exist? Which are external (structural), which are internal (person), which are time-based? A constraint taxonomy is needed before the opportunity layer can be modeled. |
| A8 | What is a Department? | 🟢 | Fully defined in ontology: Body, Drive, Heart, Mind, Navigation, Values, Communal. Internal keys: Physical, ID, Emotional, Rational, EGO, SUPEREGO, Communal. Weights matrix defined. | Closed. |
| A9 | What is a Dimension? | 🟢 | Fully defined: Physical, Emotional, Rational. Answers "what the person HAS." Distinct from Department names by design. | Closed. |

## Layer B — Dynamics
*What causes CHANGE*

| # | Question | Status | Where defined | To close |
|---|----------|--------|---------------|----------|
| B1 | What causes change in a person's state? | 🟡 | Action layer: stabilize, support, clarify, distribute, amplify. Action → expectedEnergyGain, expectedLoadReduction, expectedOrientationGain. | These are recommendations, not a theory of change. What is the mechanism? Why does "support → Emotional" actually increase orientation? The causal mechanism connecting action to outcome is asserted, not grounded. Needs behavioral theory: habit formation (Duhigg), self-efficacy (Bandura), commitment devices (Ariely/Thaler). |
| B2 | What creates a Deficit? | 🟢 | Defined: max(0, dimensionPressure − dimensionInflow). Three failure types: capacity_shortage, flow_disconnection, mobilization_gap. | Closed. |
| B3 | What is Energy? | 🟡 | Used as: beforeEnergy, afterEnergy, energyRecovered. Computed from load distribution. Not fundamentally defined. | Formal definition: energy as a measurable internal resource. How is it distinct from capacity? From motivation? From physical stamina? Conservation of Resources (Hobfoll) treats energy as one of the four resource types. Philos should specify which definition it adopts. |
| B4 | What is Recovery? | 🔵 | v2 concept. Wellbeing FSM defined: Destroyed → Damaged → Fragile → Stable → Recovered. Transition effects are uncalibrated. | Calibration requirement specified in docs/philos-calibration.md §6. Blocked until real-case outcome data exists. |
| B5 | What is Collapse? | 🟡 | Band label "Collapse" for orientation score 0–25. "Collapse detected" in NoaPanel timeline. Collapse Map section in chain view. | Clinical framing identified as a manifesto violation. Needs redefinition in development terms: not "collapse" but "orientation gap" or "pre-orientation state." What does it mean in the new Philos framework (Dream → Orientation → Action → Evidence → Growth → Opportunity) when someone has not yet reached any orientation? |
| B6 | What is Balance? | 🔴 | Not defined. Referenced in "balanceGain" component of orientation score. | What does balance mean in Philos? Is it equilibrium between forces? Between dimensions? Between internal resources and external load? SDT uses "need satisfaction" as the analogous concept. Philos needs a precise definition before balance can be measured. |
| B7 | What is Momentum? | 🔴 | Appears in the Philos Shift vision: Dream → ... → Momentum → Growth → Opportunity. Not defined anywhere. | Momentum requires longitudinal data. Cannot be defined without a persistence layer. Definition should specify: what signals constitute momentum? (Completed commitments, return sessions, behavioral consistency, growth in orientation score over time.) |
| B8 | What is Leakage? | 🟢 | Defined: totalLeakage, leakageLevel, strongestLeakingDepartment. Computed from attention drain + mission pressure + personal load factor. | Closed. |

## Layer C — Human
*The PERSON*

| # | Question | Status | Where defined | To close |
|---|----------|--------|---------------|----------|
| C1 | What is a Person? | 🔴 | Not defined in the ontology. `personStore.ts` and `computePersonChain` exist in code but with no formal definition of what a "person" is in the Philos model. | Formal definition required: what properties constitute a person in the system? At minimum: needs, values, current state (dimensions), history (events), constraints (structural). This is prerequisite to the evidence and growth layers. |
| C2 | What is Identity? | 🔴 | Not defined. | Does Philos have an identity model? Identity is distinct from current state: it is the pattern that persists across states. Options: Narrative Identity (McAdams) — identity is the story a person tells about themselves. Values-based identity — identity is the stable set of values a person acts from. Philos's "potential before recognition" implies an identity that exists before it is visible to others. This needs a definition. |
| C3 | What is Potential? | 🔴 | Central to the Philos Shift ("discover potential before recognition") but not defined anywhere. | Most important undefined concept in the system. Potential is unactualized capacity for growth or contribution. Requires: a theory of what it is, how it differs from demonstrated ability, and how it can be detected before it is expressed. Key frameworks: Zone of Proximal Development (Vygotsky), Growth Mindset (Dweck), Self-efficacy (Bandura). If potential cannot be detected before it is expressed, the core product claim fails. |
| C4 | What is Development? | 🔴 | Not defined. Appears in "Human Development Platform" positioning and in Philos Shift. | Developmental psychology has many competing models. Piaget (stages), Erikson (crises), Kegan (orders of mind), Vygotsky (social construction). Philos must choose or synthesize. Without a development model, "growth" and "evidence of growth" cannot be specified. |
| C5 | What is Trust? | 🔴 | Referenced as: "Evidence → Trust → Reputation → Opportunity" chain in Philos Shift. Not defined in the ontology. | Trust has multiple dimensions: competence-based (I trust you to do it), benevolence-based (I trust your motives), integrity-based (I trust your values). Each requires different evidence. Which dimension is Philos building trust along? In the context of "potential before recognition," trust is primarily competence + integrity trust between a potential-holder and an opportunity-holder who has no prior relationship. |
| C6 | What is Reputation? | 🔴 | Referenced in the vision chain. Not defined. | Reputation is a social construct: what others believe about a person based on observed evidence. Key questions: Whose observation? What evidence counts? Over what time window? How does it update? The architectural critique (earlier in this session) identified the verification paradox: reputation requires independent verification, but the person controls the record. This paradox is open. |
| C7 | What is a Dream? | 🔴 | First node in the new Philos flow: Dream → Reality → Obstacles → Orientation → First Action. Not defined. | In the Philos engine, a Dream is the declared intended future state of a person. It functions as the direction vector against which current forces and obstacles are measured. Without a formal definition, the new entry point ("What are you trying to build?") has no engine representation. |

## Layer D — Collective
*The NETWORK*

| # | Question | Status | Where defined | To close |
|---|----------|--------|---------------|----------|
| D1 | What is a Community? | 🟡 | Communal department exists. communityPct metric exists. The value network (helpers) exists. Not formally defined as a concept. | Formal definition: a community in Philos is the set of people who share sufficient value affinity with a person to be eligible carriers of their load. This is computable (aff(p,c) > threshold) but the threshold is undefined and the concept has no ontology entry. |
| D2 | What is Collaboration? | 🔴 | Not defined. | Does collaboration appear in Philos? Currently the value network is one-directional: helpers carry load for the subject. Collaboration implies bidirectional value creation. For the opportunity layer, collaboration is the mechanism by which potential-holders create value that opportunity-holders can observe. Needs definition. |
| D3 | What is Contribution? | 🔴 | Referenced in the Manifesto: "meaningful human impact." Not defined. | Contribution is an output, not just an action. It implies that what the person did created value for others that those others recognize. This connects to the evidence layer: a contribution is an action with a visible social trace. How Philos detects and records contribution is undefined. |
| D4 | What is Influence? | 🟡 | Influence score exists in NexusShell (node.value in the graph). But "trust comes before influence" is in the Manifesto, implying influence should be derived from trust, not stand alone. | The current influence score is ungrounded (a numeric property on graph nodes). It predates the Manifesto. The conflict between "influence ranking" as a UI element and "trust comes before influence" as a design principle is unresolved. |
| D5 | What is Opportunity? | 🔴 | Last node in the Philos vision chain. Not defined. | Formal definition: an opportunity is a specific available path from a person's current state to a more realized potential state, requiring action by the person and access provided by an external structure. It has: a source (who offers it), a condition (what qualifies the person), and an evidence requirement (what the source needs to see). Without this definition, the marketplace (Phase 3 of the business model) cannot be specified. |
| D6 | What is the Structure? | 🔴 | The external structure — the system in which a person is embedded — is not modeled. The strategic research lab (earlier in this session) identified this as the most significant gap: Philos models only internal forces while the barriers to opportunity are primarily structural (network access, institutional bias, resource inequality). | Define: what external forces act on a person from the structure? What structural elements can change vs. are fixed? How does Philos model the difference between a person who is not oriented and a person who is oriented but structurally blocked? |

## Layer E — Scientific
*VALIDATION*

| # | Question | Status | Where defined | To close |
|---|----------|--------|---------------|----------|
| E1 | How is each concept measured? | 🟡 | Calibration requirements defined for: burden, capacity, affinity, duration δ, recovery effects (docs/philos-calibration.md). Measurement definitions for most other concepts: absent. | For each concept in layers A–D: write an operational definition (observable measure), a reliability estimate, and a validity test. Priority: Potential (C3), Trust (C5), Opportunity (D5). These are the concepts the product most needs to measure. |
| E2 | How is each concept falsified? | 🔴 | Not addressed anywhere in the codebase or docs. | Science requires falsifiability. For each core claim in Philos, define the evidence that would disprove it. Example: "Value affinity predicts carrying capacity" — falsified if affinity score does not predict outcomes better than chance. If a claim cannot be falsified, it is not a scientific claim. |
| E3 | What already exists? | 🟡 | Partial: Self-Determination Theory, Conservation of Resources, Wellbeing literature identified in strategic research. | Full literature review required for: Potential detection (Vygotsky, Dweck, Bandura), Trust development (Mayer et al.), Reputation systems (reputation literature, credentialing), Opportunity allocation (social mobility research, network theory), Human development models (Kegan, Erikson). |
| E4 | Where does Philos differ from existing systems? | 🟡 | Differentiation from LinkedIn: potential vs. past achievement. Differentiation from therapy apps: development vs. relief. Differentiation from productivity tools: orientation vs. output. | Precise differentiation matrix needed. For each relevant system (LinkedIn, Woebot, BetterUp, Khan Academy, 16Personalities), define: what it measures, what it misses, and what Philos provides that it does not. |
| E5 | What is the weight matrix based on? | 🔴 | The Department → Dimension weight matrix (Body: 0.70 Physical, 0.15 Emotional, 0.15 Rational; etc.) is asserted without grounding. | Either: (a) derive from SDT / Conservation of Resources theoretical mappings, or (b) acknowledge as a provisional prior and commit to updating via calibration data. If an investor or academic asks "why 0.70?", there must be a defensible answer. |
| E6 | What is the 6-force model based on? | 🟡 | Loosely derived from Freudian structure (ID, EGO, SUPEREGO as department internal keys) combined with operational observation. Not cited, not validated. | Option A: Anchor in Self-Determination Theory (3 basic needs). Simpler, validated. Option B: Retain 6 forces but establish theoretical derivation. Option C: Treat as original model, publish validation data. The Freudian naming (ID, EGO, SUPEREGO) is a liability — both scientifically (not validated in modern psychology) and culturally (pathological connotations). |
| E7 | What is the orientation score formula based on? | 🟡 | Components: balanceGain, energyRecovery, collectiveDistribution, leakageRelief. Weights are asserted. | Same problem as the weight matrix: the formula is internally consistent but externally ungrounded. What should orientation predict? If orientation score does not predict actual life improvement, it is a vanity metric. |

## Layer F — Product
*EXPRESSION in the product*

| # | Question | Status | Where defined | To close |
|---|----------|--------|---------------|----------|
| F1 | What is the correct entry question? | 🔴 | Current: "What's weighing on you?" — identified as manifesto violation (deficit framing). New vision: "What are you trying to build?" — not yet implemented. | The new entry question must be answered by the same engine. The engine currently receives Event + Direction + Context and returns dominantForce. A Dream-first entry requires: what format does the engine receive a Dream in? How does it classify forces relative to a declared Dream rather than a declared problem? |
| F2 | What is the evidence layer? | 🔴 | Described in Philos Shift but not implemented or formally specified. | Minimum specification needed: what counts as evidence? (Commitment-and-verification loops: person declares action, system records, person reports outcome.) What makes it trustworthy? (Behavioral consistency over time, independent attestation, objective verification where possible.) What does it look like in the product? |
| F3 | What is the retention mechanism? | 🔴 | Not defined. Current product is a one-session orientation experience. The business model requires longitudinal engagement across months to generate an evidence record. | The gap between first session and evidence record is unbridged. The commitment-and-verification loop (F2) is a candidate retention mechanism: the person returns to report on what they committed to. Without a pull mechanism, retention collapses after the first session. |
| F4 | How does the product distinguish Orientation from Advice? | 🟡 | The manifesto says "understanding comes before advice." Identified violations: firstMove in ThirtySecond gives advice from one text input. "Recommended action" in Analysis view implies decision-making. | Formal product rule needed: at what point in the person's engagement has enough understanding occurred that a first move is appropriate? Currently: immediately after one text entry. Required: a minimum understanding threshold before any action is suggested. |
| F5 | What is the business model? | 🟡 | Phase 1 (evidence building, free), Phase 2 (institutional partnerships), Phase 3 (marketplace). Identified in strategic research lab. | Phase 1 requires: retention mechanism (F3), evidence layer (F2). Phase 2 requires: institutional partnerships and outcome data. Phase 3 requires: both sides of the marketplace to exist simultaneously. Critical metric for Phase 1: 30%+ of users return after 14 days to report on a commitment. |
| F6 | Who is the first user? | 🟡 | "Young people in distress" identified but problematic: highest need, lowest retention probability. Strategic research lab recommended: second/third quartile potential — high potential, some resources. | Define first user more precisely: age range, situation, what they have tried before Philos, what they would pay for, how they would describe success. Without this, product decisions have no anchor. |

## Dependency Map

Open questions that block the most other questions:

```
C3 (What is Potential?)
  → blocks C4 (Development), D3 (Contribution), F2 (Evidence layer)
  → blocks the core product claim

C1 (What is a Person?)
  → blocks C2 (Identity), C3 (Potential), the entire longitudinal layer

D5 (What is Opportunity?)
  → blocks F5 (Business model Phase 3)
  → blocks the marketplace

F2 (What is the Evidence Layer?)
  → blocks F3 (Retention), F5 (Business model)
  → blocks everything downstream of Action in the vision chain

D6 (What is the Structure?)
  → blocks the external axis of the causal model
  → without this, Philos cannot distinguish "disoriented person"
    from "oriented person in a blocked structure"
```

## Priority Order

Questions to answer first, in order of what they unblock:

1. **C3 — What is Potential?** Core product claim. If undefined, the mission statement is hollow.
2. **F2 — What is the Evidence Layer?** Second half of the causal chain. Without it, the product ends at Action.
3. **C1 — What is a Person?** Prerequisite for longitudinal tracking and the evidence record.
4. **A1 — What is a Force?** Replace Freudian framing, ground in SDT. Prerequisite for defensible classification.
5. **D5 — What is Opportunity?** Required before Phase 3 of the business model can be specified.
6. **D6 — What is the Structure?** Required before Philos can distinguish internal from structural barriers.
7. **F1 — What is the correct entry question?** New entry point has no engine representation yet.
